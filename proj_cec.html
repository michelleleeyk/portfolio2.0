Website Redesign for the Climate Entrepreneurs Club

Redesigned the website of the Climate Entrepreneurs Club such that it more accurately portrayed the club's brand image and goals. A month after the launch, the new website had a conversion rate of 15%, attracting 30 new members. Ease of obtaining information, intuitive navigation and the visual language of the website were prioritised throughout the process.

SkillsWeb Design, Branding

Project TypeVolunteer - individual

View at >www.climateentrepreneurs.uk

How I Fulfiled/Enhanced the Functions of the Website

Function 1: To provide value to existing club members
I created two new sections on the website - a resources page that provides useful information for budding climate entrepreneurs and a jobs board that advertises internships at our partner start-ups.

Function 2: To give prospective members an overview of the club and attract members
To encourage new member sign-ups, I emphasised the "JOIN US" button and placed it strategically on the navigation bar on top. Previously, the button was only on the front page and can be easily missed among a paragraph of text.

Function 3: To demonstrate the club's activity to potential sponsors
I created an section that displays the upcoming events on our home page. Since the club is highly active on social media, I highlighted this by including our recent posts on Instagram in the website footer.

Process

Upon analysis and gathering feedback from the committee members, we came to a conclusion that the visual language of the website did not align with our club image. Thus, the branding of the club was analysed before any design decisions were made.
